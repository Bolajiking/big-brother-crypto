# Star Factor — Message System (v1, for review)

**Status:** Draft. Not committed. For Bolaji's review and markup.

**Purpose:** Single source of truth for how Star Factor talks. Everything that gets written — homepage, watch page, apply, deck, brief, sponsor pitch, social — flows from here.

## 1. The category claim

> **Star Factor is the first platform for Staked Entertainment.**

**Used loud on:** investor brief, pitch deck, press, founder narrative, "About" page (when it exists).
**Used subtly on:** homepage (one mention in a sub-hero), apply page (once, contextually).
**Never used on:** the `/watch` page proper, microcopy, CTAs, social posts. Product surfaces sell the verb (Watch. Predict. Earn.), not the category.

**Why we coin it:** the a16z 2026 thesis names "staked media" as a category for credibility-via-skin-in-the-game in commentary. Star Factor extends that to entertainment — owned content where every party has a verifiable stake. Coining the label gives investors a clean shelf to put us on.

**Citation rule:** in deck and brief, footnote a16z (Hackett, Big Ideas 2026 Part 3). On viewer surfaces, never cite — borrow the language ("skin in the game", "stake", "stakeholders") without attribution. We own it through use.

## 2. The product spine

> **Watch. Predict. Earn.**

Locked. Periods, not interpuncts. Three verbs, one breath. Reads on a billboard, on a t-shirt, on a phone notification. Every surface defers to this line.

When we need a longer line, the canonical extension:

> **Watch the house. Predict the drama. Earn from your takes.**

**Wordmark rule:** the lowercase italic *starfactor.* is the **logotype** — reserved for the brand mark (header, OG image, social avatar, deck cover). In body copy, write **Star Factor** (Title Case). Don't mix the two on the same surface unless the lowercase form is acting as a logo, not a word.

## 3. Voice ladder — hybrid base + flavour by audience

We have three voices. Every surface picks **one base** and adds **one flavour** for ~10–20% of the lines (kicker, CTA, footnote).

### Voice A — Lagos-truth
Direct, confident, swaggers without trying. Sentences land hard. Naija cadence — short clauses, comma-stops, the occasional pidgin-adjacent rhythm. Speaks to a viewer, not a customer. **No corporate language. No crypto language. No hype.**

> *"You watched all night. You ran the campaigns. You built the stars. Now you get a cut."*
> *"Same drama. Different deal. You earn when you're right."*
> *"If you're going to watch all night, get something out of it."*

### Voice B — Magazine-tech (a16z-shaped)
Clean, declarative, thesis-led. Reads like a Stratechery / a16z post. Sentences earn the right to be there. Numbers used sparingly but precisely. **No hype words. No exclamation marks.**

> *"Star Factor is the first platform for staked entertainment — live content where audiences have skin in the game, creators earn in real time, and outcomes are settled in front of the camera."*
> *"Two markets exist side by side. One built the audience. The other built the willingness to spend. Nobody connected them."*

### Voice C — Game-show urgency
ALL-CAPS for live moments only. Time-bound. High-energy. Used sparingly — once per surface at most, on the CTA that *must* be tapped right now.

> *"LIVE EVICTION · MARKET CLOSES 19:00 WAT"*
> *"BACK A CAST · SUNDAY"*
> *"TYPE /PREDICT"*

### How to combine

Pick one base. Add one flavour. Never mix all three on a single surface.

| Surface | Base voice | Flavour | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage | A (Lagos-truth) | B for the credibility line under the hero | Viewers land first. One nod to investors makes the page work for both. |
| `/watch` (live + idle) | A | C on time-bound CTAs only | Native voice for the product. Game-show energy when the show demands it. |
| `/apply` | A | C on the deadline + B for the "what we offer" block | Talent reads like a viewer. Production-style urgency where it matters. |
| Investor brief / deck | B | A for two or three pull-quotes that prove we know the audience | Credentialed by default. Native voice proves we're not pure outsiders. |
| Brand/sponsor page | B | A for the audience-portrait block | Brands need numbers and audience truth. |
| Diaspora-targeted surface | A + B (50/50) | — | Diaspora reads with elevated taste. Lagos-truth carries home, magazine carries credibility. |
| Social (X, IG, TikTok) | A | C on the hook | Native voice. CTA gets the urgency. |
| Press / interviews | B | A for any quoted line from Bolaji | Sounds like a tech founder when it's structural, sounds Nigerian when it's personal. |

## 6. Glossary — words to use, words to ban

### Use freely (viewer surfaces)

| Word | Why |
|---|---|
| **Stake / stakes** | Doubles as the currency name and the philosophy. |
| **Skin in the game** | Borrows the a16z thesis without naming it. |
| **Back / call / take** | Verbs for what fans do. *Back a cast. Lock your call. Drop a take.* |
| **Predict** | Always — never "bet" on viewer surfaces. |
| **Earn / cash out / claim** | Outcome verbs. Concrete, no jargon. |
| **House / room / cast** | Show vocabulary. Owns the BBN-adjacent space without saying BBN. |
| **Heat / drama / wahala** | Native flavour. Use sparingly, never on investor pages. |
| **Lights-on / lights-out** | Show beats. Clear time anchors. |
| **Settle / settled** | The moat word. *Settled on camera. Settled in front of you.* Carries credibility weight. |
| **Live** | The most important word in the brand. Use it everywhere it's true. |
| **Stakeholder** (sparingly, mostly investor + brand) | Carries the a16z framing without saying "investor". |

### Ban (viewer surfaces)

| Word | Replacement |
|---|---|
| Crypto | (omit entirely — no replacement needed) |
| Blockchain | (omit) — or *"transparent settlement"* on credibility surfaces |
| Web3 | (omit) |
| On-chain | *"settled in front of you"* |
| Solana, USDC | (omit on viewer surfaces; use freely on investor) |
| Token | *Stakes* (the in-product currency) |
| Wallet | *Balance* or *stash* |
| Smart contract | (omit — talk about *automatic settlement*) |
| Bet / betting / gambling | *Predict / back / call / lock in* |
| Users | *Fans / viewers / the house* |
| Customers | *Fans* |
| Disrupt / revolutionize / leverage / ecosystem | (banned everywhere — magazine voice doesn't need them) |
| Pay-to-play | (banned — implies the wrong thing) |
| Monetize (about fans) | *Earn / get a cut* |

### "BBNaija" — when to name, when to imply

| Surface | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Cast recruitment (`/apply`)** | **Name it.** It sharpens the value: *"BBNaija makes you famous after. Star Factor pays you while."* |
| **Brand / sponsor pitch** | **Name it.** Brand managers benchmark against BBN. We compete on price + data. |
| **Investor brief / deck** | **Name it** — but in structural terms (their cost base, their cash cow), not as a villain. |
| **Homepage / `/watch` / social to viewers** | **Imply.** Use *"the country's biggest reality show"*, *"the show you've been watching for years"*, *"every Sunday eviction"*. Naming them on viewer surfaces sounds petty. |
| **Press / interviews** | **Imply by default. Name when asked.** Always frame as *"different layer, not replacement"*. |

### Hedge / regulatory surfaces only

Use the careful phrasing on terms-of-service, FAQ legal pages, and any document a regulator might read:
- *"Fan engagement predictions"* (not "betting markets")
- *"Skill-based forecasting"* (not "gambling")
- *"Contestant support tipping"* (not "monetization")

### a16z language — borrow without naming

These phrases come straight from Hackett / Dixon / a16z and are now ours by use:
- *"Skin in the game"*
- *"Stakes you can verify"*
- *"Settled in front of you"*
- *"Audiences are stakeholders, not eyeballs"*
- *"Trust, with a receipt"*

When we need to cite (deck, brief), the citation:
> *"Concept of staked media, Robert Hackett, a16z Big Ideas 2026 (Dec 2025)."*
